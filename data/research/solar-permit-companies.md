## 1. Government & Nonprofit Programs

### SolarAPP+ (Solar Automated Permit Processing)
- **Organization**: National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) / U.S. Department of Energy (DOE)
- **What They Do**: Web-based platform that automates residential solar and energy storage permitting for local governments (AHJs). Installers enter system design specs; SolarAPP+ conducts automated code-compliance checks and issues permits instantly.
- **Website**: https://solarapp.nrel.gov / https://www.gosolarapp.org
- **Key Products/Services**:
  - Automated plan review and instant permit issuance for code-compliant solar systems
  - Free for local jurisdictions
  - Integration with existing government permitting platforms (Accela, Symbium)
- **Adoption**: 300+ jurisdictions in 17 states; 18,906 permits processed across 150 local governments in 2023
- **Notable Partnerships**:
  - DOE Solar Energy Technologies Office (SETO) - $695K initial funding
  - IREC (founding partner)
  - SEIA
  - Accela (government permitting integration)
- **Funding**: DOE-funded through NREL
- **API/Integration**: Integrates with Accela and other government permitting software; API-accessible for solar platform integration
- **Impact**: Reduces full permitting timelines by ~14.5 days (31% reduction); direct/indirect permitting costs reduced from ~$7,000/system

### IREC (Interstate Renewable Energy Council)
- **What They Do**: Nonprofit that leads SolSmart program, founding partner of SolarAPP+, maintains Solar Licensing Database, advocates for streamlined permitting and interconnection
- **Website**: https://irecusa.org
- **Key Programs**:
  - SolSmart program (co-led with ICMA)
  - SolarAPP+ support, training, and local government outreach
  - Solar Licensing Database
  - Shared Renewables advocacy
- **Notable Partnerships**:
  - DOE SETO (primary funder)
  - NREL (SolarAPP+ partner)
  - ICMA (SolSmart co-lead)
- **Funding**: $1.2M DOE award (Jan 2025) for developing consensus recommendations
- **API/Integration**: N/A (policy/advocacy organization)

### SolSmart Program
- **Organization**: Led by ICMA and IREC, funded by DOE SETO
- **What They Do**: National designation program encouraging local governments to adopt solar-friendly policies, streamline permitting, and reduce soft costs
- **Website**: https://solsmart.org
- **Key Programs**:
  - Bronze/Silver/Gold/Platinum designations for communities
  - Free technical assistance for local governments (paused as of Oct 2025 due to funding changes)
  - Points for SolarAPP+ adoption
- **Adoption**: 600+ designated communities across 44 states, DC, USVI, and Puerto Rico
- **Goal**: 1,000 designated communities by 2027
- **Funding**: DOE extended program through 2027 in 2022
- **API/Integration**: N/A

### SEIA (Solar Energy Industries Association)
- **What They Do**: National trade association advocating for solar industry policy, including permitting reform
- **Website**: https://seia.org
- **Key Programs**:
  - SolarAPP+ advocacy and support
  - Permitting policy reform lobbying
  - Solar Market Insight Reports
  - Prediction: US to double solar installations to 6M by 2026 (with SolarAPP+ help)
- **Notable Partnerships**: NREL, DOE, state legislatures
- **Current Concern**: 74% of 2026-2030 pipeline (113 GW) at risk due to unstable permitting policies

### NABCEP (North American Board of Certified Energy Practitioners)
- **What They Do**: Gold standard certification body for solar professionals (installers, designers, inspectors, sales)
- **Website**: https://www.nabcep.org
- **Key Certifications**:
  - PV Installation Professional (PVIP)
  - PV Design Specialist (PVDS)
  - PV Installer Specialist (PVIS)
  - PV Technical Sales (PVTS)
  - PV Commissioning & Maintenance Specialist (PVCMS)
  - PV System Inspector (PVSI)
  - Energy Storage Installation Professional (ESIP) - NEW 2025
  - 3 Associate credentials

## 10. State-Level Permitting Automation

### California - SB 379
- **Law**: Senate Bill 379 (Wiener, 2022)
- **Requirement**: Non-exempt cities and counties must implement online solar permitting that verifies code compliance and issues permits in real time
- **Compliance Deadlines**:
  - Cities 50K+ pop / Counties 150K+ pop: September 30, 2023
  - Cities 5K-50K pop: September 30, 2024
- **CalAPP**: $20M grant program for local government adoption (funds exhausted)
- **Software Used**: SolarAPP+, Symbium, or custom in-house solutions

### New York
- **Status**: Fastest total solar approval timeline nationally (25 days average)
  - Permit processing: 7 days
  - Pre-installation interconnection: 5 days
  - Inspection: 10 days
  - Post-installation connection: 3 days
- **Legislation**: Considering legislation similar to California SB 379

### New Jersey
- **Status**: Passed bill to streamline and modernize residential solar permitting (late 2025)
- **Follows**: Texas model for permitting reform

### Texas
- **Status**: Passed bill to streamline residential solar permitting

- **Program**: Automated Permit Processing for Solar (APPS) grant

## 12. Key Industry Trends for 2026

### Market Context
- **Residential ITC expired** January 1, 2026 - massive shift to TPO (lease/PPA) models
- **TPO dominance**: Companies with commercial ITC access (GoodLeap, Palmetto LightReach, EverBright, IGS) are critical partners
- **Tariffs**: Up to 3,400% on SE Asian panels; domestic content compliance now essential
- **Industry consolidation**: Multiple bankruptcies (Mosaic, Sunnova, Sunlight Financial) and acquisitions

### Software Trends
1. **AI everywhere**: Every major platform adding AI design, proposals, customer sentiment, lead scoring
2. **Instant permitting expanding**: SolarAPP+ and Symbium growing; California SB 379 creating compliance pressure
3. **Platform consolidation**: Aurora acquired Lyra + SolarDesignTool + HelioScope; Enact acquired PVComplete
4. **Open API as differentiator**: Enerflo and Aurora leading with comprehensive APIs
5. **VPP software rising**: Lunar Energy Gridshare, Sunrun BrightPath enabling distributed energy
6. **TPO financing integration**: Every design platform now integrating lease/PPA options
7. **Whole-home electrification**: Platforms expanding beyond solar to batteries, heat pumps, EV charging
